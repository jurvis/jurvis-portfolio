"Truly Mobile Audio Guides"

Audio Tours remain a common way tourists use to navigate through an attraction but the experience of using one so far remains clunky an uninteractive. A user holds a clunky plastic device with uncomfortable earphones - making the experience really dull.

Created with the purpose of enhancing the feature of existing audio tour systems, ExhibitGuide is an iOS app I worked on during my internship at buUuk. The app utilizes iBeacons to track a user's position within an exhibition, following that then proceeds to feed the relevant exhibit data to the userâ€™s phones.

Using proximity-based information gathered from Bluetooth iBeacons, this app displays the relevant information of the nearest exhibit based on the user's current context. Providing a richer and seamless experience, without the user having to do much of the work himself at all.

With that, users can now use their own mobile phones and earphones to view and interact with nearby exhibits and it's metadata. Also with embedded audio recordings, the user can also convenient tap on the icon on the top right to listen to a recording of the body text.
